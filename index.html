Hi, I'm Josh

I'm an aspirant Web Dev who's learning the skills that you want your people to learn - informed by lessons that you don't want to have to teach.

My Story

What I lack in career accolades and higher education, I make up for in rich life experiences. My path has been nontradtional to say the least, with my 20's being a whirlwind of creative ambition and dead end jobs, dotted with occasional troublemaking. My background is in music and video, having made my first video at 8 years old and my first song at 17. Though I still enjoy the arts (and can still kick a sick freestyle on command), part of suceeding is knowing when to quit. I pursued my goals to a logical conclusion and went on a cross-country and international sabbatical.

Holiday Road and Viva Mexico

In May of 2020 I left Los Angeles and began volunteering on farms across the US using a platform called Workaway. It pairs penny pinching and adventurous travelers with property and land owners who are looking for help on the cheap. Eventually, I decided to travel to Mexico and take the same approach, though with less of a focus on rural farms, instead opting to work at various hostels. Increasing lockdown restrictions saw me stuck in Mexico, and I ended up living there for nearly 2 years. Last October, my Dad ran into some health issues, so I moved to Texas to help him tend to his land. Thanks to my parents generosity, I have considerable free time to alter my life trajectory by learning to code.

Why Should You Give Me A Chance?

Your way is my way

Unless part of my duties for you as a programmer include making lattes, it's unlikely that I'll have an informed approach to the work I'm doing. While I spend almost all of my free time learning to program on my own, my entire conception of how to program will be filtered through a lense that you, as my manager, establish. You can rest assured that any pre-existing systems and best practices will be observed with 0 issue.

The softest skills

Besides a high school diploma, I boast a doctorate from The University of Hard Knocks. I've experienced the failure that precedes growth. I've earned the humility that follows overzealous ambition. I've brushed shoulders with people from all walks of life: consummate professionals who boast multiple Grammy awards, and off-grid UFO shamans living in earthen domes. Collaboration, communication, and temeperance are skills that can't be taught.

No surprises

A more senior or credentialed employee might be able to use jargon or past successes to drum up their skill set, and in doing so compromise the integrity of your systems. But, Since I'm still learning, and because my resume which could fairly be deemed "uncompetitive", there's no space for me to misrepresent my skill set. You can rest assured that what you see is what you get.

Personal Interests

I still casually produce music in a variety of genres and dabble with Adobe Suite programs for visual media. I enjoy travel and hope to touch every continent in my lifetime. Besides programming and doing all manner of landscaping duties for my Dad, I enjoy weightlifting. I enjoy reading about philosophy and spirituality. I hope that, once establishing a career, I can begin plotting to have a big family.
